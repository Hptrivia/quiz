Sample Questions

  • What was the name of the disciple who replaced Judas Iscariot?
  • What is the name of the festival in John 7 where Jesus went up to Jerusalem?
  • Which prophet saw a vision of the Lord in the temple with seraphim crying “Holy, holy, holy”?
  • Who anointed David as king?
  • Which book contains the story of Balaam and his donkey?
  • What was the name of the man Jesus raised from the dead in John 11?
  • Which New Testament letter deals extensively with spiritual gifts, love, and orderly worship, including the famous "love chapter"?
  • Which Old Testament book ends with a warning about the coming "day of the Lord" and the promise that Elijah will come before it?
  • Which New Testament letter repeatedly describes believers as "in Christ" and emphasizes unity of Jew and Gentile in one body?
  • Which book focuses heavily on wisdom sayings, many attributed to Solomon?

Bible trivia works well because it covers a huge range of recognisable material, from Genesis and Exodus to the Gospels, parables, kings, prophets, apostles, and major miracles. It gives casual players plenty of entry points while still leaving room for harder questions on people, places, sequences, and specific events.

A strong Bible quiz can draw from creation, Noah, Abraham, Moses, David, Solomon, the prophets, the life of Jesus, the disciples, and the early church, alongside commandments, books of the Bible, famous verses, symbols, and locations. That range helps the theme feel fuller than a narrow religious round, because questions can shift between narrative memory, character recognition, teaching, chronology, and general knowledge that many players have picked up over time.
