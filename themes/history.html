Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • The Congress of Vienna took place after which major conflict? Napoleonic Wars
  • Which Asian war began in 1950 and ended in 1953 without a formal peace treaty? Korean War
  • Which empire was ruled by Ashoka the Great? Maurya Empire
  • Who was Hannibal’s father who also fought Rome? Hamilcar Barca
  • Who signed the Edict of Milan granting religious freedom in the Roman Empire? Constantine the Great
  • Which war was sparked by the invasion of Poland in 1939? World War II
  • The Treaty of Tordesillas divided the New World between which two countries? Spain and Portugal
  • Who was the primary author of the U.S. Constitution? James Madison
  • Which leader is associated with the term 'Cultural Revolution'? Mao Zedong
  • Who was Prime Minister of the UK during the Falklands War? Margaret Thatcher

History trivia works well because it can jump across ancient civilisations, famous rulers, battles, inventions, political change, and modern global events without running out of material. It gives players a category that feels serious and wide-ranging rather than repetitive.

A strong history quiz can include ancient Egypt, Greece, and Rome, medieval kingdoms, empires, revolutions, world wars, explorers, presidents, kings and queens, independence movements, major treaties, and social or technological turning points. The theme works because it is not tied to one era, so rounds can balance very famous events with broader historical knowledge about how societies, leaders, and conflicts shaped the world over time.
