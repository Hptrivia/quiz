Sample Questions

Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • Which beverage giant’s real-life commercial is heavily implied to be Don’s final creative triumph in the series finale? Coca-Cola
  • Which major international hotel magnate becomes one of Don’s most prestigious clients? Conrad Hilton
  • Sterling Cooper is said to have been founded in what year? 1923
  • How much money does Don offer his half-brother to convince him to leave and not come back? $5,000
  • How does Don Draper obtain his identity? He swaps identities during the Korean War
  • Which character remarks to Don, “The only thing keeping you from being happy is the belief that you are alone”? Anna Draper
  • How many total episodes aired across the series? 92
  • What is the name of the creative competitor firm that merges with Sterling Cooper Draper Pryce? Cutler Gleason and Chaough
  • On which TV network did Mad Men originally air in the United States? AMC
  • Which character is primarily known for her signature red hair in the show? Joan

Mad Men trivia works because the show is full of sharply drawn characters, memorable pitches, status games, marriages, affairs, and workplace shifts tied to a very specific era. Its details are quieter than action-driven shows, but they are often just as memorable.

A strong Mad Men quiz can include Don, Peggy, Roger, Joan, Betty, Pete, Sterling Cooper and its later forms, advertising campaigns, office hierarchy, family strain, identity questions, and the wider backdrop of 1960s American culture. The theme works because it can move between character psychology, business ambition, period detail, social change, and the smaller personal moments that give the series its depth.
