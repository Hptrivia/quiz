Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • Which German physicist is known as the father of quantum theory? Max Planck
  • Which German state capital is Hannover the capital of? Lower Saxony
  • What is the name of Germany’s upper chamber representing the states? Bundesrat
  • Which German city is famous for the Stock Exchange area and is a major banking hub? Frankfurt
  • Which Austrian-German actor starred in Inglourious Basterds and won an Oscar for it? Christoph Waltz
  • Which German company with three stripes is famous for sportswear? Adidas
  • Which German city is famous for the “Reeperbahn” nightlife district? Hamburg
  • Which German state capital is Munich the capital of? Bavaria
  • Which German train station is the busiest by passenger traffic (commonly cited)? Hamburg Hauptbahnhof
  • Which German city is the seat of the Federal Constitutional Court (Bundesverfassungsgericht)? Karlsruhe

Germany trivia works because the country gives you strong material across geography, history, science, culture, football, major cities, and national identity. It can move from Berlin and Munich to reunification, composers, inventors, and institutions without running out of recognisable ground.

A strong Germany quiz can include federal states, major cities, rivers, landmarks, the Berlin Wall, reunification, Oktoberfest, football, transport, industry, philosophers, composers, scientists, and political structure. That gives the theme depth beyond tourism facts, because players can be tested on both modern Germany and the wider historical and cultural legacy that makes the country so prominent in European trivia.
