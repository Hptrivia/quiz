Sample Questions

Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • During what years was the Zodiac Killer active as commonly cited? 1968–1969
  • Which case involves an unidentified hijacker who parachuted from a plane with ransom money in 1971? D.B. Cooper
  • Which U.K. serial killer was known as the "Yorkshire Ripper" and operated mainly in Northern England? Peter Sutcliffe
  • BTK stands for which three words (as used by the killer in communications)? Bind, Torture, Kill
  • Which piece of evidence famously linked David Berkowitz to a Son of Sam crime scene? A parking ticket
  • The “Green River Killer” was the nickname of which killer? Gary Ridgway
  • Which U.S. killer was known for attacking people in their homes and became the subject of the Netflix documentary "Night Stalker"? Richard Ramirez
  • Which true-crime documentary series focused on Robert Durst and became famous for its finale moment? The Jinx
  • Which of these is a true-crime book about the Manson Family murders? Helter Skelter
  • Which famous true-crime book by Truman Capote focuses on a Kansas family murder case? In Cold Blood

True crime trivia works because the category draws on cases people recognise from news, documentaries, books, and wider cultural discussion. It combines criminal acts, investigations, courtroom outcomes, and the details that made certain cases notorious.

A strong True Crime quiz can include serial killers, famous unsolved cases, kidnappings, frauds, police work, forensic evidence, confessions, trials, sentencing, and the media attention that turned certain investigations into major public stories. The theme works because it can move between criminals, victims, locations, investigative breakthroughs, and the wider history of how real cases were pursued, interpreted, and remembered.
