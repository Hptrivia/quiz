Sample Questions

Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • What is the name of the unique whistled language used in La Gomera, Spain? Silbo Gomero
  • What is the term for the study of how language varies across geographic regions? Dialectology
  • Which Indian language is considered the oldest and is still used in Hindu rituals? Sanskrit
  • Which Austroasiatic language is the official language of Cambodia? Khmer
  • Which extinct language was used by the Hittite Empire? Hittite
  • Which native North American language has the most living speakers? Navajo
  • Which famous 20th-century linguist proposed the theory of Universal Grammar? Noam Chomsky
  • Which phonological process involves the omission of a sound between two words, as in 'I'm gonna go'? Elision
  • Which early writing system used wedge-shaped marks on clay tablets? Cuneiform
  • Which extinct language was deciphered using the Rosetta Stone? Egyptian hieroglyphs

Language trivia works well because it can move between word origins, grammar rules, common expressions, sentence structure, meanings, and the way people actually use language every day. That gives it more life than a dry classroom-style category.

A strong language quiz can include parts of speech, synonyms, antonyms, idioms, prefixes, suffixes, grammar terms, roots, punctuation, borrowed words, and famous language facts. The subject stays playable because many answers feel familiar even when players cannot explain them perfectly, which creates a good balance between instinctive recall and proper knowledge.
