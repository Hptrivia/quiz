Tap an answer to reveal it.

  • Which language uses the Cyrillic alphabet? Russian
  • Which phonetic phenomenon occurs when one sound becomes more like a nearby sound? Assimilation
  • Which term refers to a morpheme that must be attached to another to convey meaning? Bound morpheme
  • What does the Latin phrase 'carpe diem' mean? Seize the day
  • Which of these is not one of the major languages spoken in Nigeria? Urhobo
  • Which extinct European language influenced Old English vocabulary through Viking contact? Old Norse
  • What is a glottal stop? An abrupt closing of the vocal cords
  • Which indigenous language is famous for having a dozen different words for snow? Inuktitut
  • What linguistic term describes the gradual replacement of lexical items over time? Lexical diffusion

Language trivia works well because it can move between word origins, grammar rules, common expressions, sentence structure, meanings, and the way people actually use language every day. That gives it more life than a dry classroom-style category.

A strong language quiz can include parts of speech, synonyms, antonyms, idioms, prefixes, suffixes, grammar terms, roots, punctuation, borrowed words, and famous language facts. The subject stays playable because many answers feel familiar even when players cannot explain them perfectly, which creates a good balance between instinctive recall and proper knowledge.
