Sample Questions

  • True or False: The capital of Malaysia is Putrajaya.
  • True or False: Water boils at 100°C (212°F) at sea level.
  • True or False: A square has four equal sides.
  • True or False: The mitochondrion has its own DNA separate from nuclear DNA.
  • True or False: A year on Mars is longer than a year on Earth.
  • True or False: The capital of Kazakhstan is Almaty.
  • True or False: The currency of South Korea is the won.
  • True or False: Sound travels faster in air than in water.
  • True or False: The human body has 206 bones (in adults).
  • True or False: The Great Barrier Reef is the largest coral reef system in the world.

True or False works well because the format is instantly understandable and keeps the pace moving. It can be used across almost any topic, and the challenge comes from how believable a statement sounds rather than from long question wording.

A strong True or False quiz can include history, science, geography, sport, entertainment, animals, and weird facts presented as short statements that feel obvious until players have to commit to an answer. The theme stays useful because it relies on clarity, speed, and misdirection, making it ideal for quicker rounds that still test real knowledge.
